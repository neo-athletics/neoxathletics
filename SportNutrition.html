Sport Nutrition

Nutrition plays an important role in the post, intra, and pre-of your workout, even on your days off you must be aware of what to consume. The food we eat is composed of three macro nutrients protein, carbs and fats. Each playing an essential role in the human body. Like the saying goes you are what you eat. That is why we must aware of what we eat on a daily basis. Then aside from those macronutrients we have our micronutrients. Which are composed of vitamins minerals and trace minerals. We will discuss the essential role these nutrients have in our body as well as inside and outside of training.

Macro Nutrients

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates the main energy source for the human body. Its importance has been deteriorating in the past recent years cause new trendy diets/lifestyle. But do we really understand the important role this macro nutrient has on the body. Also, the importance of carbohydrates to an athlete. First of let’s talk about carbohydrates in general what purpose do they serve in the human body and what is it composed of. We obtain the majority of our carbohydrates from plants. Such as vegetable and fruits. So, there are different kind of carbohydrates, we have our sugars, starches, and fibrous foods. For some people when they hear sugar, they hear danger., but that isn’t always the case. Not all sugars are bad for you. Let’s say fruit it has sugar and it is called fructose. But isn’t fruit good for you at least that’s what you been told right. So why will you think all of a sudden sugar is bad for you then? Maybe in large amounts it may be, but do you really know how many grams of carbohydrates should you be consuming? Well that is all based on your activity level. You shouldn’t expect to be okay consuming large amounts of carbohydrates and expect not to gain weight, at the end of the we eat to fuel our body, but if we give our body more than it can use than guess where that unused energy is going. Well one thing for sure it is not going to disappear. It will go into a conversion of being stored into fat, because the body had no use for it.

Fats

Lipids also known as fats, the one thing some of us hope to have less of in our bodies. Has its role in the performance of an athlete? Yes of course who would have though that fats help out, but they do. Believe it or not these fats help protect our organs against trauma. So, fat has its role in the human body. But those that mean we should shove fat all over our face. No, we still have to know how much and what type of fats we should be intaking. Just like carbohydrates. There are different types of fats. Which will be covered later on. Just know that some fats are able to be used as a source of energy for the human body during prolong exercise.

Different types of fat

  • Polyunsaturated fat (good fat)
  • Monounsaturated fat (good fat)
  • saturated fat (bad fat)
  • trans fat (bad fat)

When is the optimal time to take it?

Try to take fat around your day keep them light during the time you are going to work out.

Sources of this macro nutrient?

E.g. peanuts, salmon, and coconut oil.

In moderation both mono and poly improves HDL (good cholesterol) and reduce risk of heart disease Trans fat (bad fat)-stay away from it. Saturated fat (bad fat)- keep at a minimal

1 gram of fat = 9 kcal.

Some of us over do it with the protein. We either consume too much of it or not enough. Protein is used to help in muscle recovery. There are different forms of protein. There are fast acting proteins and slow digesting ones. No, I’m not just talking about your traditional whey isolate and casein protein. I am referring to foods that provide you with protein and have various speeds of absorption of protein. Like milk and Greek yogurt. They both are slow digesting proteins, but milk is partially fast acting protein around 20%. Some fasting food source protein will be eggs.

Body composition

Now let’s talk about muscle mass to fat ratio and other body measurements that are used to calculate body fat percentage and overall your health based on your height, age and weight. There are a couple of devices out there to calculate your body fat percentage and your muscle mass as well as other information. So, there is electrical impedance pretty sure some of you are familiar with it. It’s a small device where you hold it with hands and it sends electrical impedances through your body allowing to distribute the current through you muscle and estimate total body water. Which can be used to estimate fat free body mass with the difference of body weight and body fat. That’s one way to get an estimate of your body fat percentage. There are several more ways to identify your body fat percentage all with advantages and disadvantages.

Methods of calculating BodyFat %

  • Skinfold

    Measurements of subcutaneous fat with a caliper. Gives an estimation on lean body mass and fat mass.

  • Hydrostatic weighing

    Underwater weighing based on Archimedes principle to estimate lean body mass and fat free mass.

  • Air displacement plethysmography

    Also known as bod pod. Measurement of air displacement to estimate lean body mass and fat mass.

  • Bioelectrical impedance analysis

    Measurement of resistance to an electrical current to estimate total-body water, lean body mass and fat mass.

The advantage and disadvantages

Skinfold

  • Pros
  • Easy to do
  • No need of expensive equipment
  • Cons
  • Prone to error if individual is obese
  • There will be discomfort due to pinching of the skin
  • 3.5% error margin

Hydrostatic weighing

  • Pros
  • Accurate
  • 1-2% error margin
  • Cons
  • Difficult to find
  • There is a fee
  • Longer procedure

Air displacement plethysmography (Bod Pod)

  • Pros
  • Accurate
  • 1-2% error margin
  • Easy testing and fast
  • Variety of population
  • Cons
  • Fee
  • High end equipment
  • Not easily accessible

Bioelectrical impedance analysis (BIA)

  • Pros
  • Easily accessible (most gyms have them)
  • No fee (most likely)
  • Cons
  • Less accurate compared to other methods even the skinfold.
  • 4% error margin

Weight management

How many times do you hear people say I don’t get fat cause I have a fast metabolism? Do they really have a high metabolism? maybe they are only eating a meal or two a day and those two meals may be big ones but at the end of the day they end up meeting their caloric needs just at equilibrium. Meaning they won’t gain or lose weight. Heck they might even be eating less than they expect. Then the same goes to those saying they don’t eat much but the two meals they eat can contain a good 1500 calories each and there for probably have them a surplus. You can be eating little but if those foods are high in calories than there is no point in trying to starve yourself if you are going to get those calories right back in your body in a couple of hours and even go overboard. So that brings us back to the idea of eating too much of something good such as the fruit may not be always good for you if you don’t put it to good use. So that is why I always say eat for what you are going to do next. Well at least prepare your body for any intense workout or strenuous activity by providing it the fuel it needs. Like somethings in life you need balance.

What happens if you take too much of it? when is it too much? When the body has more than enough energy to use the excess amount will be stored as fat. When it is too much you are most likely at a surplus. So, if you are looking to gain weight that may be a goal you may look in to.

To gain weight be at a surplus to lose weight be at deficit and to maintain weight be at equilibrium.

1 pound is equal to 3500 kcal a week. Ex. Gain a pound by consuming 3500kcal more per week. Spread those 3500kcal for 7 days and you’ll get 500 kcal. Get 500kcal above your maintenance per day for 7 days in order to gain a pound of fat. To lose a pound you’ll be consuming 3500 less per week. So, spread that out through 7 days and you’ll be cutting 500 kcal per day to lose a pound of fat. Safe to lose 1 to 2 pounds a week.

Common methods to reach weight loss

Fasting- going prolong hours without eating

Energy restrictions-having low-calorie diets. Giving your body less calories than it needs, making your body look out for some other source as energy either stored fat our muscle.

low carbohydrates-consuming less carbohydrates in you diet preventing insulin from spiking from simple sugars or having too much glucose in the blood stream.

Increase physical activity- you can always burn more calories by doing some sort of sport or a walk at the park if you don’t want to hit up the gym.

Resting metabolic rate (RMR)- the rate in which your body burns energy when it is at complete rest.

Energy expenditure-the amount of energy your body needs to carry out your daily activities and to keep all those functions of your body running properly. If your daily workout increases so should your caloric intake in order to balance out the energy input and energy needed. That will be your TDEE.

Decrease in RMR with weight loss-you know when you start losing weight at the beginning it is going well and as you progress you start seeing a decrease and then all of a sudden you can no longer lose as much weight you did before then you hit what most people call a plateau. That is your body becoming efficient when it comes to weight loss. Also, maybe because of a loss of muscle mass during cutting season. since more muscle mass does mean a greater RMR.

Mistakes during weight loss

lose weight too fast

how wonderful will it be if we can lose weight quickly without the downfalls. wouldn’t that be a dream come true yes it will be but sadly it doesn’t happen like. At most ties it is accompanied by water loss instead of weight and a decrease in performance.

lose weight during on season

some of us make the mistake of cutting off weight during our on-season. The season where we are training intense. Now ask yourself how one can perform at its best without the proper nutrition. Yes of course motivation and dedication can take you there but what’s all that if you can’t even function properly. Then having your body get pounded and not giving it the proper nutrition makes one more prone to injuries.

not eating breakfast or lunch

if you are not fasting then I don’t recommend you skipping breakfast or lunch. your body isn’t receiving enough calories already since you are probably burning some of them at the gym or cutting some calories from other meals. you are certainly not helping your body by skipping any more meals.

taking in too little carbohydrates

what’s the first mistake some of us do when starting a weight loss goal. We drop carbs out of our diet drastically. We think it’s fine, but our bodies don’t. Yeah sure you may drop a couple of pounds the first week or so, but that may be mostly water weight. Then once you start depleting your body from carbs you may lose a couple of pounds then and there, but do you know what else drop? Your concentration and performance. You may even feel moody at times. Cause guess what you know those sugars you cut off yeah does are the ones that gives that spike we occasionally need. Besides the caffeine of course.

Eating disorders

Believe or not there are disorders in sports such as those who starve themselves and become anorexic. Also start throwing up thinking it will prevent them from gaining weight, yes it probably will but the effects of it out weight the results. Think about your health first before doing anything strenuous to your body. There are many of other disorders out there maybe someone you know may be going through it. Who knows if they really are given them the best advice you possibly can.

Anorexia nervosa

Small intake of food and concern about body weight. Sometimes having the fear of eating because they don’t want to gain weight or think they are fat and don’t want to gain any more. Even though they are already super lean.

Signs

  • Rapid weight loss
  • Dehydration
  • Feeling fatigue even if no training was done
  • Hypothermia
  • low resting heart rate
  • Muscle weakness
  • Avoiding of eating and absence from meal time

Bulimia nervosa

Affects individuals around the normal weight range. They will binge eat through the day secretly and will occasionally disappear to disperse of the contents before the nutrients are absorbed. Other times they will make up form the fact they binged on food by fasting for long hours or working out excessively.

Signs

  • Watch for the fingers if they have callus or sores
  • Dehydration
  • Dental or gum problems
  • Gastrointestinal problems
  • Muscle cramps or weakness
  • Depression
  • Secretive eating and agitation when caught binging
  • Excessive use of laxative or diuretics
